What a free plan review is

Every Medicare Advantage plan sets its own network of doctors and its own list of covered medications (called a formulary). A free plan review checks both of those things by name, before anything else.

Your doctors, checked first

We check the doctors you see today against each plan's network directory, one by one. If a plan doesn't include a doctor you want to keep, it's ruled out — as of today's information.

Your medications, checked by name

We go through the medications you currently take and see how each plan handles them. Coverage tiers and rules vary by plan, so we look at the specifics rather than general descriptions.

A plain-language walkthrough

No jargon, no rushing. We explain what we find in plain language so you understand your options clearly before you make any decision.

No cost, no obligation

The review itself doesn't cost anything and doesn't commit you to anything. You decide what, if anything, happens next.

Medicare Advantage, in plain language

Medicare Advantage plans are offered by private insurance companies approved by Medicare, as an alternative way to receive your Medicare benefits. Each plan sets its own network of doctors and hospitals, and its own formulary — the list of medications it covers and the rules around them.

Two plans can treat the very same doctor visit or the very same prescription differently. That's why a plan review looks at your specific doctors and medications by name, rather than relying on general plan descriptions or ratings. Coverage details can change from year to year, so what's checked is what's accurate as of today — not a guarantee for the future.

As an independent insurance agent, Michael represents a limited number of insurance companies rather than every plan on the market (see the disclosure below). The goal of a review is simply to show you clearly how the available options compare, so you can make an informed decision.
