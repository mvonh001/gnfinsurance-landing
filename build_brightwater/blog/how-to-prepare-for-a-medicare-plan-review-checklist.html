How to Prepare for a Medicare Plan Review: A 10-Minute Checklist

Ten quick things to gather beforehand so the conversation goes straight to what matters.

July 21·3 min read
By Michael von Heesen — Licensed Insurance Agent, Brightwater Insurance Group

A free plan review works best when it starts with good information. None of this takes long to pull together — most people can do it in about 10 minutes — but it makes a real difference in how much ground you can cover during the review itself.

The checklist

  1. List your current doctors. Name and specialty for each one — primary care, specialists, anyone you see regularly.
  2. List your current medications. Name and dosage if you have it handy. Prescription bottles or a pharmacy printout both work fine.
  3. Find your Medicare card, or current plan card. If you already have coverage, having the card in hand speeds things up.
  4. Note the pharmacy or pharmacies you use. Some plans handle preferred pharmacy networks differently.
  5. Think about any upcoming procedures or specialist visits. If something's already scheduled, it's worth mentioning.
  6. Write down your top two or three priorities. Cost predictability, keeping a specific doctor, extra benefits, travel flexibility — whatever matters most to you.
  7. Have your county or zip code handy. Coverage options vary by area, so this narrows things down quickly.
  8. Know where you are in your enrollment timing. Just turning 65, reviewing existing coverage, or dealing with a life change like retiring — this affects which options are available to you right now.
  9. Write down your questions. Even ones that feel basic. A review is a good place to ask them.
  10. Set aside 20 to 30 minutes. Reviews don't need to be rushed, and there's no obligation to decide anything on the spot.

What happens during the review itself

Once you've got the list above, the review itself is straightforward: your doctors get checked against network directories, your medications get checked against formularies, and you get a plain-language walkthrough of how your options compare — with your priorities driving which details matter most. Nothing about the review requires you to commit to anything.

If you don't have everything on the list

Don't let a missing item hold you back from starting. If you're not sure of an exact dosage or you can't immediately find your card, that's fine — bring what you have, and the rest can usually be filled in during the conversation.

Why the prep matters more than it seems

None of these ten items are complicated on their own, but together they're exactly the information a plan review depends on. Reviews that start without a doctor list or medication list still work — they just take longer, and some of that time gets spent tracking down details instead of talking through options. A few minutes of prep beforehand tends to make the conversation itself shorter and more useful, not longer.

How long does a Medicare plan review take?

Most reviews take about 20 to 30 minutes, though it can run longer if you have a lot of medications or questions. There's no need to rush.

Is there any cost for a plan review?

No. A plan review is free and comes with no obligation to enroll in anything.

Do I have to enroll in a plan if I do a review?

No. A plan review is informational. You decide afterward what, if anything, you want to do next.
