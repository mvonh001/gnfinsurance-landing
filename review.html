Not all doctors and medications are covered the same

Every Medicare plan has its own doctor network and its own formulary — the list of medications it covers and the rules around them. Two plans can treat the very same doctor visit or prescription very differently. A free review shows you how the options line up with the doctors you see and the medications you actually take.

The doctors you want to keep

Every plan has its own network, and not every doctor is in it. We check your doctors by name against each plan's directory before anything else — if a plan doesn't include them, it comes off the list.

Blood thinners like Eliquis

A common prescription such as Eliquis can sit on different coverage tiers depending on the plan. A review checks whether your plan handles it the way you expect, so there are no surprises at the pharmacy counter.

Diabetes medications like Jardiance

Coverage rules for medications like Jardiance vary from plan to plan — some require extra steps before they are covered. Knowing this ahead of time helps you compare your options clearly.

Newer medications like Ozempic

Widely prescribed medications like Ozempic are not treated identically everywhere. A plan review shows how your medications are covered so you can make an informed choice for the year ahead.

About Bay Harbor Benefits

Bay Harbor Benefits is an independent Medicare agency based in Bay Harbor Islands, serving all of Miami-Dade County — led by Michael, a licensed Florida agent with more than 10 years helping people navigate healthcare costs. Michael doesn't work for one insurance company — he works with six of them, including Humana, UnitedHealthcare, and Aetna. That's what independent means: your doctors and medications get compared across the plans available in Miami-Dade, and the recommendation follows what fits you, not a sales quota.
